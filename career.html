Peace Corps in Colombia
April 2019 - Present

I’m currently a Peace Corps Volunteer serving as a Business Advisor in Colombia. The program I’m participating in focuses on promoting economic development in the northern coastal region of the country. This translates into giving entrepreneurship and personal finance classes, organizing and facilitating community savings groups, supporting a nonprofit’s micro-loan program, and advising small businesses and community associations in my site.

I also work in areas related to Peace Corps Colombia’s secondary objectives, namely youth and technology. I organize a boys’ leadership and development club and a girls’ empowerment club in my site. In regard to technology, I have an after-school tech club that focuses on developing typing skills and basic digital literacy. I’m also a member Peace Corps Colombia’s ICT4D (Information and Communication Technology for Development) committee which aims to use technology to enhance Peace Corps Volunteers’ work in Colombia’s economic and educational contexts.

Data Scientist in DC
January 2018 - March 2019

From January 2018 to March 2019, I was a Data Scientist in Washington, DC. I worked at Booz Allen Hamilton and was on a project supporting the U.S. Department of Labor’s financial system. Initially, I diagnosed and resolved issues experienced by users of the financial system as a Financial Systems Analyst. During this time, I initiated and built an application that automates data integration tasks and performs diagnostics for common user issues.

Shortly thereafter, I became a developer on my project. While in this role, I initiated and built a tool that automates the migration of reports between software platforms and that facilitates the development of new reports that are generated by the financial system. This tool initially saved about 70 man-hours during a reporting modernization initiative requested by the Department of Labor. It’s also a resource that will be used to develop new reports in the future.

UrbanPromise Wilmington
June 2017 - December 2017

UrbanPromise Wilmington is a non-profit that puts on summer camps and after-school programs for inner city kids in Wilmington, DE, a city with one of the highest murder rates per capita in the US. I was an intern at this organization for 7 months from June through December 2017. During the summer, I was on staff at a summer camp for children in elementary school. In the fall, I was an Assistant Director at an after-school program for middle schoolers.

While I was at UrbanPromise, I built an interactive map showing the organization’s presence amidst all the violence in Wilmington. This map was used in fundraising literature and presented during the keynote speaker's speech at the organization’s annual banquet. I also designed and implemented a survey to collect data on outcomes of UrbanPromise’s teen job training program. I then used this data to produce statistics and data visualizations for a grant application that led to the receipt of a $50K award.

William & Mary
August 2012 - May 2017

I graduated from William & Mary with a BA in Economics in May 2016. My coursework emphasized quantitative skills (math and econometrics) and foreign language study (Spanish and French). During my time as an undergrad, I took jazz piano lessons and studied abroad in Spain twice.

Participating in this program sparked my interest in data science and artificial intelligence. For my capstone project, I utilized machine learning and natural language processing to classify international development projects into one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals set by the UN.
